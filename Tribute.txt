<div id="tribute-info">
<h3>Biography</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>1856</strong> -- Born to Galician Jewish parents in the Moravian town of Freiberg, in the Austrian Empire.</li>
<li><strong>1881</strong> -- Qualified as a Doctor of Medicine at the University of Vienna.</li>
<li><strong>1891</strong> -- Published his first book "<em>On the Aphasis: a Critical Study</em>".</li>
<li><strong>1902</strong> -- To be made a Affiliated Professor in Neuropathology at the University of Vienna.Also this year, founded his discussion group <em>Wednesday Psychological Society</em>.</li>
<li><strong>1908</strong> -- Held the first <em>International Psychoanalytical Congress</em> in Salzburg.</li>
<li><strong>1910</strong> -- Founded the <em>International Psychoanalutical Association (IPA)</em>.</li>
<li><strong>1914</strong> -- Published "<em>The History of the Psychoanalytic Movement</em>", the German original being first published in the <em>Jahrbuch</em></li>
<li><strong>1923</strong> -- Diagnosed with oral cancer.</li>
<li><strong>1930</strong> -- Was awarded the Goethe Prize in recognition of his contributions to psychology and to German literary culture.</li>
<li><strong>1938</strong> -- Published his last book "<em>Moses and Monotheism</em>"</li>
<li><strong>1939</strong> -- Died at the age of 83.</li>
</ul>
